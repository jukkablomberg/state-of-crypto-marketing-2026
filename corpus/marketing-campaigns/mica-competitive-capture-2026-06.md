## Why this is the load-bearing Theme-3 item

The 2026-06-26 / 06-27 / 06-29 longitudinal notes flagged the missing leg explicitly: the corpus had the *cessation* side (Binance's forced EU marketing pull-down) but not the *completed, named, quantified capture* side — *"whether Kraken/Coinbase/Bitvavo/Ripple visibly capture the migrating EU marketing surface"* (06-29 run-record recommendation item (e)). This run closes it. As of 1 July, the MiCA licence stops being a compliance checkbox and becomes a **live customer-acquisition weapon**: licensed Tier-1s are running dated, quantified, EEA-geofenced promotions explicitly aimed at customers of the platforms that failed to license in time. The marketing surface itself is the competitive asset the report has been arguing it would become.

**Displaced side (the targets):**
- **Binance** — withdrew its Greek MiCA application (~2026-06-24); restricting new registrations and certain EU services from 1 July; says customer assets "remain accessible." (See `../regulator-filings/binance-mica-eu-exit-2026-06.md`.)
- **Bybit Global** — said EEA access would be **progressively limited from July 1**. Entity-split nuance (net-new this run): **Bybit's European subsidiary remains MiCA-authorised via its Austrian licence** — so the "Bybit" brand is *both* a displaced entity (Bybit Global) and a licensed entity (Bybit EU). Users must confirm which legal entity holds their account. ESMA's own warning underlines this: MiCA protections attach to the **licensed European legal entity**, not automatically to every company operating under the same brand. This is a clean Theme-1/Theme-4 "gate-stack visibility" case — same brand, two regulatory states.

## Market-structure context (Theme 3 quantification)

- **244 MiCA CASP licences approved as of Monday 2026-06-29** (per Crypto Briefing). **Germany leads with 57** — the single largest national issuer. **Greece, Hungary, Poland, Portugal and Romania had issued zero.** (Greece issuing zero is directly relevant: it was Binance's chosen — and withdrawn — base.)
- OKX Europe GM **Erald Ghoos** said the exchange saw **record new customer sign-ups** ahead of the deadline. *(Logged as market-structure colour, NOT a class-4 operator statement — Ghoos is a regional GM/CEO, not a senior marketing operator (CMO/VP Marketing/Head of Brand/Growth); excluded from class 4 consistent with the 2026-06-29 run's ruling on the same speaker.)*
- Earlier OKX-shared data (cited in coverage) indicated ~60% of European crypto users were still on unlicensed exchanges weeks before the deadline — i.e., a large displaced pool the capture campaigns are competing for.

## Synthesis read (for Phase-2)

This is the cleanest demonstration of the report's central Theme-3 claim: **the MiCA licence is a marketing-surface advantage, monetised in real time.** The same regulatory event (1-July transitional-period end) produces a *symmetric pair* in the public record — (a) the unauthorised incumbent's marketing goes dark (Binance, Bybit Global), and (b) the licensed challengers convert their authorisation into aggressive, quantified acquisition spend (OKX 8% / Coinbase 5% / Kraken €1M). Both halves are publicly observable, both are primary-source-anchored, and together they validate the methodology's "visibility-as-analysis" premise: the marketing surface a regulator can verify is exactly the surface that decides Q3 competitive outcomes.
