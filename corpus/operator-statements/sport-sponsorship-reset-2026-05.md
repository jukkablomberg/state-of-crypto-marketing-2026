## Incidents (chronological)

### 1. Bybit ends $150M Oracle Red Bull Racing F1 sponsorship (Q4 2024)
- **Source:** Bybit corporate communications + Sportcal coverage Q4 2024.
- **Stated framing:** Ben Zhou — Red Bull renewal failed an internal review on conversion-to-user economics. Not "we couldn't afford it"; "the deal doesn't pay back."
- **Tenure:** Three-year deal signed early 2022; not renewed at end of 2024 cycle.
- **Theme 1 signal:** Marketing P&L review now uses conversion-to-user economics as the primary deal-evaluation lens. The CMO seat shifts toward marketing-economics defence vs. brand-deal making.

### 2. Crypto.com layoffs + AI-native framing (March 2026)
- **Source:** Crypto.com corporate communications, Bloomberg, CoinDesk.
- **Stated framing:** Marszalek — "12% reduction; AI-native restructuring; next chapter; leaner."
- **Magnitude:** ~12% headcount. Marketing function's share unannounced (becomes visible six weeks later via the Kalifowitz exit).
- **Theme 5 signal:** First Tier-1 exchange to put "AI-native" + cost-discipline in the same restructuring announcement.

### 3. Coinbase 14% layoff + Armstrong AI-native-pod memo (5 May 2026)
- **Source:** Brian Armstrong company memo May 5 2026; Coinbase Q1 earnings same week.
- **Stated framing:** AI-native pods. Five layers below CEO. Player-coaches instead of pure managers. Individual operators in IC-plus-manager seats supported by an agent stack, accountable for a number not a slide.
- **Magnitude:** ~700 staff (~14%). Q1 loss $394M same window. AWS outage same week put front-end down for hours.
- **Theme 1 signal:** First publicly listed Tier-1 to name the operating model that replaces the CMO-led brand department. Spine of the May 7 NorthPoint essay "Coinbase named the model."

### 4. Crypto.com CMO Steven Kalifowitz exit announcement (5 May 2026)
- **Source:** Crypto.com corporate communications, CoinDesk, The Block.
- **Stated framing:** Kalifowitz exits end of June; six-year tenure; succession during AI-native restructuring chapter.
- **Theme 1 signal:** Marketing function's share of the March restructuring. The $1B-sponsorship-era CMO seat ends; the replacement-seat shape unannounced.

### 5. Binance CMO Rachel Conlan exit + Eowyn Chen interim (12 May 2026)
- **Source:** CoinDesk (Ian Allison scoop) 12 May 2026; Wu Blockchain framing.
- **Stated framing:** Conlan exits 15 June (three years). Eowyn Chen (ex-CEO Trust Wallet) named interim CMO. Conlan stays on as adviser.
- **Theme 1 signal:** First Tier-1 to put a non-marketing operator (wallet product CEO) into the interim CMO seat without running a search. The brief is being re-cut into a product-and-distribution job sitting where the marketing org used to sit. Spine of the May 13 NorthPoint essay "Binance lost its CMO too."

### 6. Crypto.com Predictions head Travis McGhee exit (~13 May 2026)
- **Source:** FX News Group, 13 May 2026.
- **Stated framing:** McGhee — longest-tenured operator on the prediction-markets/derivatives line; exits the company. Third senior departure from Crypto.com in 60 days after Kalifowitz (CMO) and Lundgren (CLO, April).
- **Theme 5 signal:** Three senior exec departures in 60 days at one Tier-1 = structural leadership reset, not a single-seat succession. The Ritson critique lands inside an executive vacuum.

### 7. Tour de Suisse terminates Zondacrypto title sponsorship "with immediate effect" (~10 May 2026)
- **Source:** Inside Sport, Tour de Suisse organiser statement, Estonian FSA + Polish AML probe coverage.
- **Stated framing:** Tour de Suisse — partnership "proved unsustainable" after eight weeks. Trigger = Estonian Financial Supervision Authority investor warning + Polish fraud/money-laundering probe attached to Zondacrypto. Title was announced in March; cancelled in May.
- **Theme 5 signal:** When the sponsoring exchange runs into a regulatory event, the sport partner is exposed to brand risk and walks away on twenty-four-hour notice. The sport-celeb thesis depended on a stable-counterparty assumption that 2026 has stopped honouring. Canyon-SRAM (women's cycling) + Giro d'Italia Women sponsorships now also in jeopardy.

## Synthesis (Theme 1 first read)

Six exchange-marketing operator-statements in ~120 days converge on one structural reset:

1. **The unit of marketing work moves from a brand department reporting to a CMO to a small pod of operators reporting to a P&L line.** (Coinbase named it; Binance and Crypto.com running it under different labels.)
2. **The CMO seat itself changes shape** — from an agency-side hire with deal-making background to a product-and-licence-stack operator who can run AI-augmented pods, defend the marketing P&L against AI-skeptic CFOs, and ship compliant marketing under MiCA/FCA/SEC/HK-VATP/SG-PSP in parallel.
3. **The sponsorship line item is no longer the largest discretionary spend on the deck.** Bybit ended its largest deal on conversion economics. Crypto.com's $1B inventory is under explicit board review. Zondacrypto's deal was terminated by the partner.
4. **Agency-vendor stack silence is the structural proof.** MarketAcross holds PR retainers at Binance and Crypto.com simultaneously and has issued zero public commentary on either CMO transition in 14 days. PR retainers are not scoped to comment on succession at the level of the seat they sit below.
