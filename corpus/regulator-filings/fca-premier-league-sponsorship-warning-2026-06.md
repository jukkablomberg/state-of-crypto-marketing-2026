## What happened

The FCA publicly warned football clubs — including Premier League clubs — that sponsorship deals with **unauthorised** crypto firms and online trading platforms could expose the clubs to legal liability, money-laundering risk, and reputational damage. The FCA said some unauthorised firms may be breaching UK financial-promotion rules by using club branding to put financial products in front of fans, said it had already contacted clubs where it identified concerns, and signalled enforcement "where needed." Timing: ~8 days before the FIFA World Cup 2026 (kickoff 2026-06-11) — i.e., a deliberate pre-tournament intervention at peak football attention.

## Verbatim quote (primary)

> "Millions of football fans trust their club's badge. Clubs should not let unauthorised financial firms exploit that loyalty by putting potentially dodgy products in front of millions of fans."
> — **Lucy Castledine, FCA Director of Consumer Investments** (quoted by Reuters, 2026-06-02)

Castledine also framed the consumer-facing point: "A logo on a shirt means one thing: that firm paid for it" — not a safety endorsement — and pointed fans to the FCA's Firm Checker / warning-list tools before using any promoted financial product.
