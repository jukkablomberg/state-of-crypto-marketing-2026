## Why this is in the corpus (marketing relevance)

The longitudinal note's June read was that the regulator is policing crypto marketing at two layers (placement/channel — FCA→clubs; entity-permission/solicitation — ESMA 23-June). The open watch item was **coordinated NCA action** operationalising ESMA at member-state level. This AMF item is that leg from the bloc's strictest MiCA interpreter:

- France reiterates that, from **1 July 2026**, any entity providing crypto-asset services to EU clients **without MiCA authorisation must cease such activities** — solicitation/marketing of EU residents included (only the narrow reverse-solicitation exemption survives).
- Crucially, the AMF anchors a **France-specific wind-down clock that pre-dates the EU deadline**: following its **public statement of 5 February 2026**, the AMF states that non-authorised DASPs without a pending CASP application should have had an **orderly wind-down plan in place as of 30 March 2026**, carrying out only operations strictly necessary to settle their affairs. So in France the marketing/solicitation surface for unauthorised entities was already expected to be winding down months ahead of 1 July.
- Non-EU entities **may not provide (or solicit) crypto-asset services to EU clients** outside the reverse-solicitation carve-out; the investor warning instructs EU users to verify their provider is an authorised CASP on the AMF register and migrate before the deadline.

**Theme-4 read:** the marketing-cessation obligation is not only an EU-level expectation but a **dated, NCA-enforced** one — and France set an earlier internal clock (30 March 2026) than the headline 1-July date. For a tracked firm relying on a French surface, "marketing going dark" was a compliance expectation a full quarter before the deadline. Strengthens "marketing cessation as the leading public indicator of CASP authorisation failure" with a national-enforcement timestamp.
