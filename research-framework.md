# State of Crypto Marketing 2026 — Research Framework

> **Replaces the prior interview-guide.md (deprecated 2026-05-04 — see `interview-guide.md` for the deprecation note).** This framework defines the questions the report answers and the public-source evidence types that answer each one. Each theme below has explicit data inputs from the methodology corpus and explicit synthesis outputs in `./findings/`.

---

## Theme 1 — The shape of the marketing function today

**Question:** How is the senior marketing function organised at the 30 tracked firms in mid-2026, and where does the gate-stack seat live (or not live)?

**Public-source evidence:**
- Job postings tagged "VP / Head of / Director of" plus marketing function (brand, growth, PMM, regulatory-comms, agency-mgmt) — read for what's listed AND what's structurally missing.
- LinkedIn People filters on each tracked firm's company page — current titled marketing leadership.
- Press releases announcing new senior marketing hires or seat eliminations.
- Public org-chart references in conference talks, podcasts, or press features.

**Synthesis outputs (in `./findings/01-shape-of-the-function.md`):**
- Headcount-by-firm table, with split (brand / growth / PMM / community / regional / agency-mgmt / regulatory-comms).
- Map of where the gate-stack-equivalent seat sits — when it exists as an FTE, when it sits inside legal/compliance, when it is structurally absent.
- Distribution of "split-IC" patterns (e.g., Bybit's Apr 28-29 IC postings without a CMO seat above).

---

## Theme 2 — AI in the stack

**Question:** What AI tooling is actually changing how the function operates in 2026, versus what firms are publicly claiming about adoption?

**Public-source evidence:**
- Job postings asking for AI tooling experience (Claude, GPT, Perplexity, agent-frameworks, Anthropic API, MCP). Track by firm + month.
- Public statements at conferences and on podcasts about AI workflow adoption.
- Investor-update language about "AI efficiency" — particularly tied to layoff narratives.
- Vendor announcements claiming named crypto firms as customers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Perplexity, vertical-AI vendors).

**Synthesis outputs (in `./findings/02-ai-in-the-stack.md`):**
- Adoption-claim vs. adoption-evidence matrix.
- Gap analysis: which firms have the loudest AI narrative versus which firms have the most JD-confirmed AI tooling adoption.
- "AI-cover" classification for each 2026 layoff: AI as cause / AI as cover for cyclical reset / AI as actual restructure trigger.

---

## Theme 3 — The agency stack

**Question:** Which firms run which agencies, where are the overlaps, and which agency relationships ended in the last 12 months?

**Public-source evidence:**
- Agency case-study pages and press releases (the 18-agency competitor-intelligence panel).
- Firm-side announcements naming new agency relationships.
- Public posts ending an agency relationship (rare but valuable).
- Cross-reference: where two or three agencies all claim the same firm publicly.

**Synthesis outputs (in `./findings/03-agency-stack.md`):**
- Agency-overlap matrix (firm × agency).
- Multi-agency pattern analysis (which firm types tend to run 1 vs 2 vs 3 agencies; what predicts overlap).
- 12-month timeline of named agency-relationship endings.
- Identified governance gaps where multi-agency overlap exists without a single internal seat owning the stack.

---

## Theme 4 — MiCA and regulated-marketing readiness

**Question:** Which EU-passporting firms have publicly visible MiCA-marketing-comms work in their org, and which firms have a structural exposure surface visible only by absence?

**Public-source evidence:**
- ESMA April 17 Statement requirements.
- MiCA Commission Delegated Regulation on marketing communications (specific obligations).
- Public marketing copy on each tracked firm's site, audited against the requirements (footer disclosures, risk language, register-verification prompts, KOL-disclosure language).
- Job postings naming "MiCA," "regulated marketing communications," or "marketing compliance."
- Public regulator actions Q2 2026.

**Synthesis outputs (in `./findings/04-mica-readiness.md`):**
- Per-firm exposure-surface scorecard (footer disclosure status, risk-language status, KOL-disclosure status, jurisdiction-coverage status, regulatory-comms hire status).
- Aggregate read on which firms appear to be at lowest vs. highest risk going into July 1.
- Gap inventory: which themes the public marketing copy systematically misses across the panel.

---

## Theme 5 — The next twelve months

**Question:** What does the public-record evidence suggest about hiring direction, restructure plans, and seat creation/removal for the next 12 months at each firm?

**Public-source evidence:**
- Layoff announcements + earnings disclosures Q1-Q2 2026.
- Net hiring direction (12-month JD count trajectory per firm).
- Regional shifts in posting concentration.
- Public restructure announcements.
- Agency-relationship endings (often a leading indicator of a senior-hire-to-fill).

**Synthesis outputs (in `./findings/05-next-twelve-months.md`):**
- Net-add / flat / contraction classification per firm.
- Geographic shift map (where are senior marketing seats moving in 2026).
- Predicted 6-month senior-hire pipeline (which seats are most likely to be created or filled).
- Hiring-vs-layoff alignment with public AI-narrative honesty.

---

## How findings tie back to the report

Each theme produces:
1. A quantitative table or matrix readable in a single page.
2. A 5-7 page narrative synthesis citation-anchored to the underlying corpus.
3. A "what the visible record tells us about the invisible" closing read — what the public absence in each theme implies for firms heading into Q3 enforcement.

The five themes plus an opening framing chapter and a closing implications chapter compose the 40-page report.
