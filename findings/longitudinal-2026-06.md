## Shift flagged this month

The **sport-sponsorship reset** thesis — first coded in `../corpus/operator-statements/sport-sponsorship-reset-2026-05.md` from the operator/P&L side — gained a **regulatory-pressure leg** in June 2026:

- **Sell-side (already documented, May):** exchanges exiting sport deals on conversion-economics (Bybit/Red Bull) and counterparty-risk (Zondacrypto/Tour de Suisse) grounds; CMO seat shifting from brand-deal-maker to P&L-defender.
- **Buy-side (new this month, June):** the **FCA publicly warned the host properties themselves** — Premier League / football clubs — that taking money from unauthorised crypto sponsors is a financial-promotion / money-laundering / reputational exposure, contacted clubs, and signalled enforcement (2026-06-02/03; see `../corpus/regulator-filings/fca-premier-league-sponsorship-warning-2026-06.md`).

**Synthesis implication:** the crypto sport-sponsorship line item is now squeezed from both ends simultaneously — the buyer (exchange) is cutting it on economics, and the regulator is pressuring the seller (club) on legality. For the report's Theme 4 read, this is a clean example of the regulator policing the *channel/placement*, not just the firm's owned marketing copy — i.e., "what is publicly visible" now includes shirt/stadium inventory, not only landing pages.
